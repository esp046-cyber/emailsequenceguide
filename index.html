üìö Email Marketing 101 Fundamentals

What is Email Marketing?

Email marketing is a direct communication channel where businesses send targeted messages to subscribers who have opted in to receive them. For e-commerce brands, it's the highest-ROI channel, generating $42 for every $1 spent in 2026.

üí° WHY IT MATTERS:

Unlike social media (where algorithms control reach), YOU own your email list. When you send an email, it reaches your subscriber's inbox directly - no middleman, no algorithm changes, no platform dependency.

Key Email Marketing Terms (Glossary)

Essential Terminology

ESP (Email Service Provider): Platform you use to send emails (Klaviyo, Mailchimp, etc.)

Open Rate: % of recipients who opened your email

CTR (Click-Through Rate): % who clicked a link in your email

CTA (Call-to-Action): Button/link prompting action (e.g., "Shop Now")

Conversion Rate: % who completed desired action (purchase, signup)

Bounce Rate: % of emails that couldn't be delivered

Unsubscribe Rate: % who opted out after receiving email

Segmentation: Dividing list into groups based on behavior/traits

A/B Testing: Testing 2 versions to see which performs better

Deliverability: Ability to reach inbox (not spam folder)

Automation/Sequence: Pre-written emails sent based on triggers

Drip Campaign: Series of emails sent over time

Setting Up Double Opt-In

What is it? After signup, user gets confirmation email and must click to confirm.

Pros:

  • Higher quality list (only engaged users)
  • Better deliverability (fewer bounces/spam)
  • Legal protection (proof of consent)
  • Required in EU (GDPR)

Cons:

  • Lose 20-40% of signups (don't complete confirmation)
  • Extra friction in signup process
üí° RECOMMENDATION:

Use double opt-in if: (1) Selling to EU, (2) B2B business, (3) High-ticket items. Skip if: (1) US-only, (2) Impulse buys, (3) Need fast list growth. You can always clean list later.

Typography Best Practices

  • Use web-safe fonts: Arial, Georgia, Times, Verdana, Courier
  • Limit to 2 fonts max: One for headlines, one for body
  • Maintain hierarchy: H1 > H2 > Body > Fine print
  • Line height: 1.5x font size (18px font = 27px line height)
  • Paragraph length: 2-3 lines max on mobile
  • Avoid all caps: Harder to read, looks like shouting

Accessibility Guidelines

Make Your Emails Accessible to All:
  • Use ALT text on all images (describe what's shown)
  • Maintain 4.5:1 contrast ratio (text vs background)
  • Don't rely solely on color to convey info
  • Use semantic HTML (headers, paragraphs, lists)
  • Keep language simple (8th grade reading level)
  • Avoid flashing GIFs (seizure risk)

2 Identify Inactive Subscribers

What to do: Find people who never engage

Create "Inactive" Segment:

  • Definition: Haven't opened email in 90+ days
  • AND: Haven't clicked in 120+ days
  • AND: Haven't purchased in 6+ months (or never)

In ESP Segment Builder:

Conditions: - Last opened email: More than 90 days ago - Last clicked email: More than 120 days ago - Has placed order: Zero times (or >180 days ago) - Is suppressed: No (not already unsubscribed) Name: "Inactive 90+ Days"

Analyze Segment Size:

  • If inactive segment is >30% of list ‚Üí Major problem
  • 15-25% inactive ‚Üí Normal, needs cleaning
  • <10% inactive ‚Üí Healthy list

Why This Matters:

  • Inactive subs hurt deliverability (ESPs see low engagement)
  • You're paying for subscribers who don't engage
  • Skews your analytics
  • Reduces overall email performance
3 Create Re-Engagement Campaign

What to do: Give inactive subs one last chance

Build 3-Email Win-Back Series:

Email 1: "We Miss You"

  • Subject: "{{First_Name}}, are you still there?"
  • Content: We noticed you haven't engaged
  • Offer: Small incentive (10% off)
  • CTA: "I'm still interested" button

Email 2: "What Changed?" (Send +7 days)

  • Subject: "Did we do something wrong?"
  • Content: Ask for feedback, update preferences
  • Offer: Bigger incentive (15-20% off)
  • CTA: "Update preferences" or "Give feedback"

Email 3: "Last Chance" (Send +14 days)

  • Subject: "This is goodbye (unless...)"
  • Content: We'll remove you soon if no action
  • Offer: Best offer (25% off or free gift)
  • CTA: "Stay subscribed" button

Set Up in ESP:

  • Create campaign or automation
  • Target: "Inactive 90+ Days" segment
  • Send all 3 emails over 2-3 weeks
  • Track: Who opens, clicks, or purchases

Expected Results:

  • 10-15% will re-engage
  • 2-5% will purchase
  • 85-90% will remain inactive ‚Üí Remove these
4 Remove Bounces & Complaints

What to do: Clean out invalid emails

Hard Bounces (Remove Immediately):

  • What: Email doesn't exist, domain invalid
  • Action: Auto-suppressed by most ESPs
  • Manual check: Look for "bounced" status
  • Delete: Yes, remove permanently

Soft Bounces (Monitor):

  • What: Temporary issues (inbox full, server down)
  • Action: ESP retries automatically
  • If soft bounce 3+ times ‚Üí Treat as hard bounce

Spam Complaints:

  • What: Someone marked your email as spam
  • Action: ESP auto-unsubscribes them
  • Monitor rate: Should be <0.1% (1 in 1,000)
  • If higher ‚Üí Review content, frequency, expectations

In ESP, Create Suppression Segment:

Conditions: - Email status: Bounced (hard) - OR Marked as spam - OR Invalid email format Action: Suppress from all sends
5 Segment Active Users

What to do: Identify and reward your engaged subscribers

Create "Highly Engaged" Segment:

  • Opened email in last 30 days
  • Clicked in last 60 days
  • OR Made purchase in last 90 days

Create "Moderately Engaged" Segment:

  • Opened in last 60 days
  • Clicked in last 90 days

Segment-Based Strategies:

  • Highly Engaged: Send more often (3-4x/week okay)
  • Moderately Engaged: Normal frequency (2-3x/week)
  • Low Engaged: Less frequent (1x/week max)
  • Inactive: Win-back only, then remove

Special Treatment for Top Engagers:

  • Create VIP segment (opened 80%+ of emails)
  • Give early access to sales
  • Send exclusive content
  • Reward loyalty
